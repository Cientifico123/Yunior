🔧 CONDUIT FILL — LLENADO DE CONDUIT
📖 WHAT IS IT? / ¿QUÉ ES?
Conduit fill refers to the maximum percentage of a conduit's cross-sectional area that conductors can occupy. The NEC limits this to prevent heat buildup, insulation damage, and pulling difficulty.

El llenado de conduit es el porcentaje máximo del área de la sección transversal de un conduit que pueden ocupar los conductores. El NEC limita esto para evitar acumulación de calor y daño al aislamiento.
📋 NEC REFERENCE / REFERENCIA NEC
Chapter 9, Table 1 gives the maximum fill percentages:
• 1 conductor: 53% fill
• 2 conductors: 31% fill
• 3+ conductors: 40% fill

Annex C (Tables C.1–C.12) shows the maximum number of conductors for each conduit type and trade size.
📐 FORMULA / FÓRMULA
Fill % = (Total conductor area ÷ Conduit internal area) × 100
Each conductor's area is found in Chapter 9, Table 5. Conduit internal area is in Chapter 9, Table 4.
🔨 FIELD EXAMPLE / EJEMPLO DE CAMPO
Q: Can you run 4 conductors of #12 THHN in 1/2" EMT?

• #12 THHN area = 0.0133 in² each
• 4 conductors = 4 × 0.0133 = 0.0532 in²
• 1/2" EMT internal area = 0.122 in²
• Fill = 0.0532 ÷ 0.122 = 43.6% — ❌ EXCEEDS 40% limit

Solution: Use 3/4" EMT (area = 0.213 in²) → Fill = 25% ✅
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES / ERRORES COMUNES
⚠️ Using the wrong conductor area — always use the INSULATED conductor area from Table 5, not the bare wire area.
⚠️ Forgetting that equipment grounds count in the fill calculation.
⚠️ Mixing conductor sizes — calculate each size separately and add total areas.
📦 BOX FILL — LLENADO DE CAJAS
📖 WHAT IS IT? / ¿QUÉ ES?
Box fill is the calculation that ensures a junction or device box has enough volume to safely contain all conductors, devices, and fittings without crowding.

El llenado de caja garantiza que la caja tenga suficiente volumen para contener todos los conductores, dispositivos y accesorios de forma segura.
📋 NEC REFERENCE / REFERENCIA NEC
NEC 314.16(B) — Volume allowance required per conductor:
• #14 AWG: 2.00 in³ each
• #12 AWG: 2.25 in³ each
• #10 AWG: 2.50 in³ each
• #8 AWG: 3.00 in³ each
• #6 AWG: 5.00 in³ each
📐 COUNTING RULES / REGLAS DE CONTEO
Count each conductor entering the box. Additionally:
• Device (switch/receptacle): Count 2 conductors of the largest connected wire
• Internal cable clamps: Count 1 conductor (largest wire)
• Ground wires: All grounds = 1 conductor (largest ground)
• Fixture studs/hickeys: Count 1 each
🔨 FIELD EXAMPLE / EJEMPLO DE CAMPO
4×4×1.5 box with: 4 × #12 conductors + 1 receptacle + 1 ground

• 4 conductors × 2.25 in³ = 9.00 in³
• 1 device × 2 × 2.25 in³ = 4.50 in³
• 1 ground × 2.25 in³ = 2.25 in³
• Total needed = 15.75 in³
• 4×4×1.5 box volume = 21 in³ ✅ Passes
⚡ AMPACITY & DERATING — AMPACIDAD Y CORRECCIÓN
📖 WHAT IS IT? / ¿QUÉ ES?
Ampacity is the maximum current a conductor can carry continuously under specific conditions without exceeding its temperature rating.

Derating reduces the ampacity when conductors are bundled together or in high ambient temperatures, because heat has less room to dissipate.
📋 NEC REFERENCE / REFERENCIA NEC
NEC 310.15 | Table 310.16 — Base ampacity values at 30°C ambient
NEC 310.15(B)(1) — Temperature correction factors
NEC 310.15(C)(1) — Adjustment factors for conduit fill (4+ current-carrying conductors)
📐 DERATING FACTORS / FACTORES DE CORRECCIÓN
Conduit Fill Derating (310.15(C)(1)):
• 4–6 conductors: 80%
• 7–9 conductors: 70%
• 10–20 conductors: 50%

Temperature Correction (75°C column):
• 31–35°C: 94% | 36–40°C: 88% | 41–45°C: 82%
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES / ERRORES COMUNES
⚠️ Using 90°C conductor ampacity with 75°C terminals — always use the terminal rating column.
⚠️ Not counting all current-carrying conductors in conduit fill derating.
⚠️ Neutrals carrying harmonic currents DO count as current-carrying conductors.
⚙️ MOTORS — MOTORES ELÉCTRICOS
📖 OVERVIEW / RESUMEN
Motor circuits require four levels of protection: branch-circuit short-circuit protection, conductor protection, overload protection, and motor disconnect. Each has its own NEC rules.
📋 KEY NEC REFERENCES
Article 430 — All motor requirements
Table 430.248 — Single-phase FLC values
Table 430.250 — Three-phase FLC values
430.22 — Conductor sizing (125% of FLC)
430.52 — Short-circuit protection (OCPD sizing)
430.32 — Overload protection
📐 MOTOR CALCULATIONS STEP BY STEP
For a 10 HP, 208V, 3-phase motor:

1. FLC from Table 430.250: 30.8A
2. Conductor: 30.8 × 125% = 38.5A → Use #8 AWG THHN
3. Overload: 30.8 × 115% = 35.4A → Use 35A overload
4. OCPD (inverse time CB): 30.8 × 250% = 77A → Use 80A breaker
🔄 TRANSFORMERS — TRANSFORMADORES
📐 CURRENT CALCULATION / CÁLCULO DE CORRIENTE
I = kVA × 1000 ÷ (V × √3) [Three-phase]
I = kVA × 1000 ÷ V [Single-phase]
Example: 45 kVA, 480V primary, 3-phase:
I_primary = 45,000 ÷ (480 × 1.732) = 54.1A
📋 PROTECTION RULES / PROTECCIÓN
NEC 450.3(B) — Transformer protection:
• Primary OCPD: Not more than 125% of primary FLC
• If impedance ≤ 6%: Secondary OCPD ≤ 125% of secondary FLC
• Dry-type transformers 600V and below: may use next standard size up
🌍 GROUNDING & BONDING
📖 GROUNDING vs BONDING
Grounding connects the electrical system to the earth (ground) to stabilize voltage and protect from lightning/fault.

Bonding connects all metal parts together to ensure they stay at the same potential, creating a low-impedance fault path.
📋 KEY RULES / REGLAS CLAVE
250.24(A)(5) — Neutral and ground must be separated at ALL panels except the service entrance (main panel).

250.66 — GEC sizing based on service entrance conductor size

250.53(A)(2) — Two ground rods required minimum 6 feet apart

250.52(A)(3) — UFER ground (concrete-encased electrode) — 20 ft of #4 AWG bare copper in footing
⚠️ CRITICAL MISTAKES
⚠️ NEVER bond neutral to ground in a subpanel — this creates stray current on the equipment grounding conductor and violates 250.24(A)(5).
⚠️ The GEC must be continuous — no splices except as permitted by 250.64(C).
🎛️ PANELBOARDS — TABLEROS
📋 WORKING CLEARANCES / ESPACIOS DE TRABAJO
NEC 110.26(A) — Minimum working clearances in front of panel:
• 0–150V: 3 feet (Conditions 1, 2, 3)
• 151–600V Condition 1: 3 feet
• 151–600V Condition 2: 3.5 feet
• 151–600V Condition 3: 4 feet

Minimum headroom: 6.5 feet
Minimum width: 30 inches
📋 BREAKER HEIGHT / ALTURA DE BREAKERS
NEC 240.24(A) — OCPDs shall not be installed in bathrooms.
NEC 240.24(E) — OCPDs not to be located above 6.5 feet (2m) from the floor, except for service equipment or in a dedicated space.
📉 VOLTAGE DROP — CAÍDA DE TENSIÓN
📐 FORMULA / FÓRMULA
VD = (K × I × L) ÷ CM
Where:
• K = 12.9 (copper) or 21.2 (aluminum)
• I = Current in amperes
• L = One-way length in feet
• CM = Circular mils of conductor (from wire tables)

NEC recommends max 3% for branch circuits, 5% total (feeder + branch).
🔨 EXAMPLE / EJEMPLO
120V circuit, #12 AWG (6530 CM), 20A, 75 feet:
VD = (12.9 × 20 × 75) ÷ 6530 = 2.96% ✅ Under 3%

If 100 feet: VD = (12.9 × 20 × 100) ÷ 6530 = 3.95% — consider #10 AWG
🔌 BRANCH CIRCUITS — CIRCUITOS RAMALES
📋 RECEPTACLE PLACEMENT RULES
NEC 210.52(A) — In dwelling units:
• No point along any wall shall be more than 6 feet from a receptacle
• Receptacles required on all wall spaces 2 feet or wider
• Kitchen countertops: no point more than 24 inches from receptacle
📋 CONTINUOUS LOADS / CARGAS CONTINUAS
NEC 210.19(A)(1) — Branch circuit conductors must have an ampacity of not less than 125% of the continuous load plus 100% of the non-continuous load.

A continuous load is one expected to continue for 3 hours or more.
🔋 FEEDERS — ALIMENTADORES
📋 FEEDER SIZING RULES
NEC 215.2(A)(1) — Feeder conductors shall have an ampacity not less than needed to supply the load, and not less than 125% of the continuous loads + 100% of non-continuous loads.

OCPD must also be sized at 125% of continuous + 100% of non-continuous per 215.3.
🛡️ GFCI & AFCI PROTECTION
📋 WHERE GFCI IS REQUIRED / DÓNDE SE REQUIERE GFCI
NEC 210.8(A) — In dwelling units, GFCI required in:
Bathrooms • Garages • Outdoors • Crawl spaces • Unfinished basements • Kitchens (within 6ft of sink) • Boathouses • Bathtubs/shower areas • Sinks in other locations
📋 WHERE AFCI IS REQUIRED / DÓNDE SE REQUIERE AFCI
NEC 210.12(A) — AFCI required for all 120V, 15A and 20A branch circuits supplying outlets or devices in:
All dwelling unit areas: Bedrooms • Living rooms • Dining rooms • Kitchens • Hallways • Closets • Sunrooms and similar areas.
📋 TAMPER RESISTANT / RESISTENTE A MANIPULACIÓN
NEC 406.12 — Tamper-resistant (TR) receptacles required in all areas of dwelling units. TR receptacles have internal shutters that block insertion of foreign objects unless both slots are pressed simultaneously.
📐 WORKING SPACES — ESPACIOS DE TRABAJO
📋 TABLE 110.26(A)(1) CONDITIONS
Condition 1: Exposed live parts on one side, grounded or no live parts on the other

Condition 2: Exposed live parts on one side, grounded parts (concrete walls, etc.) on the other

Condition 3: Exposed live parts on both sides (most hazardous — widest clearance required)
⚠️ KEY REQUIREMENTS
⚠️ Working space cannot be used for storage — ever. Keep it clear at all times.
⚠️ At least one entrance of sufficient area to allow personnel to escape must be provided per 110.26(C).
